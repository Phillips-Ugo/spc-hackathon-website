About the Project

Built on the Seeed ReBot arm and the LeRobot framework, Rebot tackles two core tasks for accessibility. Object manipulation fine-tunes MolmoAct2 on 115 demonstrations to learn pick-and-place, generalizing beyond the training object to new items.

Autonomous writing uses URDF/Pinocchio inverse kinematics with dynamic contact detection — the arm monitors joint torques during descent to find the paper surface automatically, then draws multi-stroke letters with pen-up travel between characters.

Use Cases

01

Assisted Pick & Place

The arm mounts directly onto a wheelchair, letting the user pick up objects from shelves, move items between surfaces, and set a plate — without waiting for a caregiver.

02

Assisted Writing & Drawing

Attached to a wheelchair, the arm can write on paper for individuals who cannot grip a pen. Torque-based contact sensing adapts to any surface height automatically.
